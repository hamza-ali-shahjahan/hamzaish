# Hacker News Launch Playbook

## The framework in one paragraph

HN's "Show HN" is a unique channel: hostile to marketing language, brutal to bad ideas, but uniquely powerful when you genuinely have something interesting built. A front-page Show HN drives 20K-100K visits, several thousand signups for good products, and lasting credibility in tech circles. There's no upvote ring; you can't manufacture a HN launch like a PH launch. You can only optimize the title, the post body, and the launch timing — then engage every commenter for the next 12 hours.

## When to use

- After MVP works and is interesting (the bar is "interesting" + "usable")
- When you have something a builder/maker would actually want to try
- When you're comfortable getting roasted publicly — HN comments are blunt

## Pre-launch

### Build interest IN HN itself
- Have HN comments in your post history (an account with 0 prior comments looks spammy)
- Karma helps but isn't required

### Write the title
- Format: `Show HN: <product> – <what it does>` (en-dash, not hyphen — that's the convention)
- Under 80 chars
- Don't oversell. "Show HN: An AI that writes your CRM updates" beats "Show HN: The future of B2B sales 🚀"
- No emojis. No buzzwords. Plain.

### Write the post body (first comment)
- 150-300 words. Plain English. No marketing copy.
- Start with: what you built, why you built it (real motivation)
- Middle: how it works (technical detail is welcome — this is HN)
- End: what you'd love feedback on

### Don't include
- Pricing in the body (they hate ads)
- "We're disrupting..."
- "Excited to share..."
- Investor / funding mentions

### Pick the day & time
- **Best**: Tuesday-Thursday, 06:00-09:00 PT (Pacific time matters because of US engineering audience)
- **Worst**: Friday afternoon, weekend, any holiday

## Launch moment

### Submit at the time chosen
- Title + URL + body in one post
- Don't ask anyone to upvote (HN bans manipulation)
- Don't submit on multiple accounts (instant shadow-ban)

### Wait 5 minutes, then comment to expand
First comment from your same account, often a 200-word "more context" if useful.

## The first 2 hours (critical)

HN's ranking algorithm front-loads early upvotes. The first 10 upvotes in 30 min determine whether you hit the front page. So:
- Reply to EVERY comment within 5-10 minutes
- Reply substantively (don't say "thanks!" — engage with the technical point)
- Don't get defensive on critical comments — concede valid points, push back on invalid ones with evidence
- Update your product or post in real time if a commenter found a bug

## What HN responds to

- **Technical depth** — explain how you built it, what you struggled with
- **Honest tradeoffs** — "this doesn't work well for X yet because Y"
- **Open source angles** — if any part is OSS, link the repo
- **Real metrics** — "we have 200 users and 3 paying so far" beats "thousands of users"
- **A demo that works** — broken demos get destroyed
- **Anti-buzzword copy** — say what it does

## What HN destroys

- **Vague AI marketing** — "leveraging AI to revolutionize..."
- **Closed-source where OSS would be expected** — they don't have to use it, but they'll question your choices
- **Pricing pages with no free tier and high prices** — be ready to defend the pricing in comments
- **No demo link** — must work without login if possible
- **Fake-looking testimonials** — they'll find out

## Post-launch

- Save the entire thread — comments are gold for product feedback
- Tweet "we did Show HN, here's what we learned" the next day
- DM commenters who gave high-signal feedback — offer a thank you (no upsell)
- Track signups by referrer for 7 days — Show HN traffic decays in 48 hours

## What success looks like
- **Front page (top 30)**: 5K-30K visits, durable
- **Top 10**: 30K-100K visits, real signup spike
- **#1 or above-the-fold for an hour**: viral
- Not front page: gets the URL share value, much smaller traffic
