# 2026-06-19 — The test→ingest loop, and the funnel principles it surfaced

## The reusable pattern

A studied repo changes nothing until its lesson becomes (1) a concrete pass/fail **test** against our own repo and (2) an exact **destination file**. "We should learn from X" is inert; *"X teaches pattern P → here's the test that proves P fits us → here's the file P lands in"* upgrades the factory. Findings that **fail** the test are the highest-value output — they are the to-do list.

A lesson is only a *candidate* once ingested; it is **proven** only after it ships in a real product and shows an effect. Ingest in the factory, prove on the product, let the retro promote/correct/kill the playbook.

## Funnel / discoverability principles (ported)

- **Incumbent-anchored positioning as the literal headline** beats brand-led taglines for cold visitors (the 10-second test).
- **A visible release cadence is a content engine** — distribution dominates, cadence compounds it. See [`release-cadence-as-content`](../../factory/playbooks/launch-stage/release-cadence-as-content.md).
- **Stars ≠ PMF** — track fit on its own axis (Sean Ellis + retention), never a launch spike. See [`stars-are-not-pmf`](../anti-patterns/stars-are-not-pmf.md).
- **The funnel moves on *executing* public changes** (positioning, hero, demo GIF), not on writing more internal docs. Documentation is necessary, not sufficient.
