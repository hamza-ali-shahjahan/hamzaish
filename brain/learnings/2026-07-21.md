# 2026-07-21

## Analytics is a planning artifact, not a launch chore

Every product so far shipped without interaction tracking, and every time the founder had to notice the empty report and intervene — the fifth product (ThousandWorlds Explorer) made the pattern undeniable: GA4 was "added" yet the SPA reported a single `/` row for three tabs and two flagship flows, and the companion emulator app had no tag at all. The retrofit cost a full session (dual-sink `track()` helper, ~20 call sites, virtual per-tab `page_view`s, GA4 custom-dimension registration) and surfaced a data-corrupting UX bug (the landing hijacking anonymous deep-link visitors mid-session) that plan-time instrumentation would have exposed on day one. The fix is structural, now baked in: `/feature-slicing` requires a **Track:** decision per slice (event+params, page_view path, or an explicit "none — why"), the spec template gained a **Trackability** section, `/user-analytics` gained Recipe F + Definition-of-Done #7, and the go-live gate gained **A11 — Trackable**: a product that can't answer "which screens do users visit and what do they do there?" is not shareable yet.

## SPAs are invisible to page analytics by default

The auto `page_view` fires once at load; tabs, modals, and wizard steps never exist in the data unless each fires a virtual `page_view` with a real `page_path`. Corollaries that bit: multi-app products need the tag in *every* `index.html` (grep the measurement id per deployed app — "I added GA4" to one app ≠ covered); GA4 event params are invisible in reports until registered as custom dimensions; React StrictMode double-fires effect-driven events in dev only (don't "fix" it); and reading `location.search` per-render in an app that rewrites its own URL turns "arrived via deep link" into a lie mid-session.
