# 2026-07-05 — The Live Path goal: verified-live over declared-live, set-up-once made measurable

**Decision.** Adopt `meta/goals/live-path.md` as the factory's active structural goal: any scaffolded product goes from local to a *verified* live URL (A1–A10 assertions against the deployed site, not the local build) in ≤15 min of human time and ≤5 dashboard actions, with zero account-level re-setup on subsequent products. Milestone order: wire the A1–A10 eval harness into `/go-live` first (M1), then automate the four audited manual dead-ends — registrar/DNS, Resend verification, Clerk app + webhook, Neon provisioning (M2), then the account-aware ledger fast path (M3), then scaffold the kill switch (M4).

**Why.** A 2026-07-05 repo audit against the operator's vision ("set up the stack once, get 10 products live 80% of the way — real shareable products, not localhost prototypes") found the doctrine documented and the walked path proven (7 ships, Ventbox/TASFORT live), but the orchestration layer designed-not-built: `/go-live` guides rather than automates, "live" is declared rather than asserted, and product #2 repeats product #1's manual wiring. The same audit caught `stack/README.md` claiming "product #2 in ~5 minutes" — present-tense aspiration, softened same day; E3 of this goal is what earns the claim back. The README was also repositioned the same day from local-first-as-destination to local-as-mile-one ("Localhost is where products are born, not where they live").

**What would prove it wrong.** If two real ships through M1–M3 still exceed 15 min / 5 dashboard actions, the bottleneck is account signup and service dashboards themselves — i.e., not automatable from the terminal without violating the never-automate-ToS/card boundary — and the goal's bar (not its direction) needs resetting. If the A1–A10 harness produces false confidence (passes while a real user can't sign up), the assertion set is wrong, not the idea.

**Revisit trigger.** After E1+E2 pass on two products (one Supabase-path, one Neon+Clerk-path) and E3 holds across three ships → mark achieved, restore the stack README's minutes claim with ledger receipts, and open the successor goal (distribution automation or load-readiness, operator's call). Or at the next quarterly `/kill-or-keep` on Hamzaish itself, whichever comes first.
