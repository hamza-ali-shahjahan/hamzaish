# Acknowledgments

![Hamzaish — the credits roll](docs/assets/hamzaish-credits.gif)

Hamzaish is built on a thousand generosities. Almost nothing here is
wholly original — it's the field's best ideas, carefully studied, credited, and
recombined in service of one mission: **getting every builder into Builder Mode,
fast, and keeping them there.** This is the credits roll. Stay through to the
end — like the best films, there's a scene after the credits, and it's about you.

> Our one rule: **when in doubt, credit.** Credit the idea even when the license
> doesn't require it. Name the specific debt, not a vague "thanks to the
> community." A repo that remembers where it came from is one you can trust with
> where it's going.

## 🧠 The shoulders we stand on

We learn in the open. These taught us patterns we reimplemented in our own code
(we never import — we port the idea and leave a comment pointing home). If you
built one of these: thank you. You moved us forward, and we're loud about it.

- **[Addy Osmani](https://github.com/addyosmani/agent-skills)** — agent-skills: the spec → build → ship discipline at the core.
- **[Andrej Karpathy](https://karpathy.ai)** — eval-driven development and the learning flywheel (the conviction behind our whole Selection layer).
- **[Garry Tan](https://github.com/garrytan/gbrain)** — gbrain: knowledge graph + hybrid retrieval, and an eval methodology that shaped ours.
- **[Anthropic](https://claude.com/blog/the-founders-playbook)** — *The Founder's Playbook* that sharpened the Idea→MVP→Launch→Scale framing, and **Claude** + **Claude Code**, the engine this factory runs on.
- **[Nous Research](https://github.com/nousresearch/hermes-agent)** — hermes-agent: self-improving skills + memory loops.
- **[openclaw](https://github.com/openclaw/openclaw)** — multi-channel gateway patterns.
- **[ponytail](https://github.com/DietrichGebert/ponytail)** by **DietrichGebert** (MIT) — the one-skill-many-agents portability layout, and the lazy-senior-dev creed that *the best code is the code never written.*

*Frameworks woven through the playbooks: Sean Ellis (the 40% PMF signal we won't
fake), Rob Fitzpatrick's* The Mom Test, *and Clayton Christensen's
Jobs-to-be-Done. We didn't invent them; we lean on them daily.*

## 🎬 After the credits — yes, you

If you cloned this, opened an issue, fixed a typo, starred it at 2am, or just
read this far: **you're part of it now.** Builder Mode isn't a product we sell
you — it's a spirit we're handing you better tools for. Every new builder makes
the next one's path shorter.

So build something today. Credit the people who got you there. Be kind in your
issues and generous in your PRs. And when *your* repo gets studied one day — pass
it on.

**Welcome to Builder Mode. We're glad you're here.** 🚀
