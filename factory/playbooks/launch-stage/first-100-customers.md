# First 100 Customers

## The framework in one paragraph

Getting your first 100 paying customers is **manual**, **direct**, and **founder-led**. There are no "growth hacks" at this stage. The work is: define a precise audience, find them where they congregate, reach them 1:1, ship them a tight value prop, and iterate hard based on signal. The first 100 are who teach you what your product really is and what to charge — they are validation infrastructure, not just revenue.

## Where the first 100 come from (typical breakdown)

| Source | Typical % | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| Personal network (warm) | 5-15% | Friends + ex-colleagues. Don't over-weight signal. |
| Cold outreach (DM/email) | 30-50% | The dominant channel for first 100 B2B. |
| Community engagement | 15-30% | IndieHackers, niche subreddits, Discord, Slack groups. |
| Product Hunt / HN launch | 10-20% | One-time spike. Long tail from listing SEO. |
| Content / SEO | 0-10% | Usually too early. SEO compounds at customer ~100-500. |
| Referrals from early customers | 5-15% | If they exist by customer #30, your retention is right. |
| Paid ads | 0% | Don't. Not at this stage. CAC is too unpredictable. |

## The week-by-week playbook (rough)

### Week 1 — Define & list
- Lock the target profile (use `agents/idea/customer-discovery` if not done)
- Build a list of 200-500 specific people
- Stand up the outreach tracker (sheet / Airtable / Notion DB)

### Week 2-3 — First 50 outreaches
- 25/day max, split across email + LinkedIn DM
- Test 2 subject lines, 2 openers, 2 asks
- Track every reply (yes / no / no-reply / negative)
- Calibrate at 50 sends: which variant won?

### Week 4 — First demos / calls
- Target: 5-10 first conversations
- These are usually discovery + soft sell
- Record every call (with permission)
- Iterate the product based on patterns
- 1-3 should convert if your value prop is right

### Week 5-8 — Iterate outreach + product
- Keep 25/day outreach with the winning variants
- Ship product improvements based on call feedback
- Build the case study from your first paying customers
- Start asking customers for referrals + LinkedIn endorsements

### Week 9-16 — Compound
- By now, 20-50 paying customers if going well
- Add lightweight automation (Loops / Resend sequences for activation)
- Begin first content piece (SEO seed)
- Run Product Hunt launch if MVP is solid

### Week 17-24 — Hit 100
- Mix of channels now working
- One of {SEO, community, partnerships, referrals} should be growing
- Define the next 100 — different playbook depending on channel mix

## The "channels that work" decision

By customer 30, you should know which 1-2 channels are working. Double down. Cut the others.

Signs a channel is working:
- Cost per customer is dropping
- Quality of customer is acceptable (acceptable churn, good NPS)
- You can scale the channel without proportional founder time
- You can measure attribution

Signs a channel is NOT working:
- High CAC + high churn (acquired the wrong users)
- Founder time scales linearly with users (no leverage)
- Conversion is decent but the LTV doesn't justify

## The "founder-does-everything" anti-pattern

At customer 1-100, you do everything: support, sales, onboarding, demo, content, code. That's correct.

By customer 50, you should have written down the workflows you're repeating. By customer 100, you start automating or delegating the ones not worth your time. See `agents/scale/support-triage/`, `agents/launch/cold-outreach/`.

## The "pricing changes" reality

You'll likely raise prices 2-3x between customer 1 and customer 100. Common path:
- First 10 customers: discounted, or at the low end of pricing
- Customer 30: standard pricing, maybe 10% increase
- Customer 100: another 20-30% increase as value becomes clearer

Don't apologize for raising prices. Each raise comes with more proven value. Notify existing customers; grandfather them.

## Common failure modes

- **Quitting outreach at 100 sends.** Most outreach calibrates at 200+ sends.
- **No tracking.** "I'll remember who I emailed" — no, you won't.
- **Sending generic emails.** Personalize the first line, every time.
- **Treating first customers as marketing wins.** They're product validators. Listen more than sell.
- **Diluting focus across 5 channels.** Pick 1-2 first; expand only after proving them.
- **Skipping calls.** Email is great; calls are where real product feedback comes from.
- **Not asking for referrals.** Every happy customer should be asked for 1-3 referrals after week 4.

## The 100-customer post-mortem

After hitting 100, run a retrospective:
- Which channels actually worked (revenue-weighted)?
- What's the persona that converted vs the one we targeted? (often different)
- What's the median time-to-paid?
- What's M3 retention? (if < 40%, focus retention before next 100)
- What's the LTV/CAC by channel?

Save in `products/<name>/decisions/first-100-retro.md`. This is your blueprint for the next 900.
