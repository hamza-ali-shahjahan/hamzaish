# $100K ARR — Tactical Playbook

## The framework in one paragraph

$100K ARR is the "real money for one founder" threshold. The path from $0 to $100K is not a smaller version of the path from $100K to $1M — it's a fundamentally different game played at lower complexity, lower stakes, with higher iteration speed. The tactics: pick a niche too small for VC-backed competitors, charge enough that 200-300 customers gets you there, validate by hand, ship in weeks not months, build distribution before you build the product, and stay solo. This is the explicit target for products in this factory.

## The math (5 plausible paths)

| Customers needed | ARPU | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| 50 | $2K/mo | High-touch B2B, vertical SaaS, enterprise-light |
| 100 | $1K/mo | SMB B2B, agency tools, professional services SaaS |
| 200 | $500/mo | Mid-market team tools, agency lite, vertical pro |
| 400 | $250/mo | Pro/prosumer tools, dev tools, creator economy |
| 1000 | $100/mo | Consumer pro, indie SaaS, content tools |
| 3000 | $30/mo | Consumer mass-market, mobile, prosumer-light |

The higher the ARPU, the fewer customers — but harder to acquire. Sweet spot for solo: 100-400 customers at $250-$1K/mo.

## Picking the right niche

### Properties of a $100K-ARR niche
- Customer count needed: 50-1000 (not millions)
- ARPU: $30-$2K/mo (not $5, not $50K)
- Distribution: reachable by 1 person (not enterprise sales force)
- Competition: few full-time competitors (large enough to attract VC = wrong target)
- Pain: real enough that customers pay willingly
- Founder fit: you understand the user or can reach them

### Niches that fit
- Vertical SaaS for trades / industries (plumbers, lawyers, accountants, dentists)
- Productivity / workflow tools for specific roles (newsletter writers, podcasters, course creators)
- Compliance / admin tools for regulated industries
- AI-native versions of existing tools (where AI 10x's the value)
- Internal tools sold as products (you built it for yourself; others want it)

### Niches that don't fit (for $100K target)
- Anything venture-scale (need to hit billions, not $100K)
- Marketplaces (cold start kills solo founders)
- Consumer mobile (CAC kills bootstrapped)
- Enterprise software (long sales cycles, big team)

## The 12-month $100K plan (template)

### Month 1-2: Validation
- Define ICP precisely
- 30 customer discovery interviews
- Lock the hypothesis
- Build prototype as a conversation prop

### Month 3-4: MVP
- Ship minimum-viable in 4-6 weeks
- 5 first customers (likely from discovery interviews)
- Charge from day 1 — even $10/mo

### Month 5-6: First 25 customers
- Manual outreach, 25 sends/day, 5 days/week
- Iterate product on every conversation
- By customer 25: ~$1.5K-$5K MRR

### Month 7-9: First 100 customers
- Find the channel that's working, double down
- Raise prices on new signups
- Start one content piece per month (SEO seed)
- By customer 100: ~$5K-$15K MRR

### Month 10-12: Scale to 200-400
- The working channel scales (with help: VA, automation, content cadence)
- Add referral / viral mechanics if applicable
- Raise prices again on new signups
- $100K ARR = $8.3K MRR; achievable at this stage

This is aspirational; many products take 18-24 months. The factory's job is to compress this.

## Tactics that work at this scale

### Charge upfront / annual
Better cash flow, lower churn, simpler ops. Bake in 15-20% annual discount.

### Done-for-you bundles
Charge for the result, not the tool. Add a service tier for the first 10-30 customers (yes, even if you're "just a SaaS"). The interactions teach you what to build.

### Lifetime / one-time payment options
Controversial but works for some products. Indie users love it; you lose recurring revenue. Use sparingly.

### "Be the user" marketing
Your social presence demos your product. Pieter Levels' brand IS Nomad List + Photo AI. You don't have a marketing department; you have a Twitter account.

### Reverse trials
Start the user with full features (paid), downgrade after 14 days unless paid. Conversion rates can be 3-5x freemium.

### One feature, polished to obsession
Better to do one thing exceptionally than three things adequately. Pick the wedge.

## Tactics that DON'T work at this scale

- **Cold paid ads** — CAC > LTV at indie scale
- **Sales-led B2B with quotas** — needs full-time AEs
- **PR / press** — minimal traction unless you're in tech press already
- **Free tiers (most cases)** — drains support, low conversion
- **Hiring a "marketing person"** — you can't manage if you don't know the channel yourself

## Common failure modes

- **Building before validating** — most $0 ARR products were built without first 5 customer conversations
- **Pricing too low** — $10/mo for B2B leaves money on the table; ICP undervalues you
- **Building too long pre-launch** — 3+ months of stealth build = 3 months of learning lost
- **Founder ego on niche** — "but the market is bigger if we generalize" — no, focus
- **Quitting at month 4** — most products take 6-12 months to hit clear signal; quitting at 4 is premature

## When $100K is enough vs when to push for more

$100K ARR for a single founder is ~$70-85K take-home depending on costs — comparable to a senior engineering salary. Considerations:
- **Stop and run another product (the factory play)**: more diversification, more freedom
- **Push to $500K with the same product**: requires marketing investment but no team
- **Push to $1M with a small team (2-5)**: requires hiring + management — different game
- **Acquihire / acquisition**: $100K-$300K ARR products often acquire at 2-5x ARR multiples

For this factory: hit $100K, then either push to $500K or kick off next product (per `agents/portfolio/kill-or-double-down/`).
