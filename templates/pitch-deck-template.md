# Pitch Deck Template — {{PRODUCT_NAME}}

10 slides. Use only if you decide to raise (most $100K-ARR products won't).

## Slide 1: Title
- Product name
- One-sentence positioning
- Stage + ask (e.g., "Pre-seed, $500K")

## Slide 2: The problem
- What pain are you addressing? Be specific.
- Who feels it?
- How big / frequent / costly?
- Current workaround and why it fails

## Slide 3: The solution
- One sentence
- One screenshot or demo GIF
- Bullets: 3 key benefits

## Slide 4: Why now
- The 2026 enabling trend (AI, regulation, behavior change)
- Why this couldn't have been built in 2020

## Slide 5: The market
- TAM/SAM/SOM (with sources)
- Bottom-up calculation preferred
- "Who has budget" view of the market

## Slide 6: Product
- 3-4 screenshots of core flows
- 1-line caption each

## Slide 7: Traction
- MRR / paying customers / signups (whichever is strongest)
- Growth rate (week-over-week or month-over-month)
- Cohort retention curves
- Testimonials (1-2 strong)
- Logo wall if relevant

## Slide 8: Business model + GTM
- Pricing tiers
- Acquisition channels working
- Unit economics: CAC, LTV, payback

## Slide 9: Team
- Founder(s) + relevant background
- Why this team / why this problem
- Advisors / past wins

## Slide 10: Ask
- $ amount
- Use of funds (% breakdown)
- Milestones the round funds (what's true 18 months out)
- Contact info

## Appendix slides (only if asked)
- Competitive landscape (table)
- Detailed financials / model
- Roadmap
- Customer case studies
- Patent / regulatory status

## Design notes
- Sans-serif font, ≥ 24pt body, ≥ 36pt headlines
- High contrast (dark text on white preferred)
- One idea per slide
- Number every slide
- Footer on every slide: product name + page #
- Use product UI screenshots, not stock photos
- < 10MB file size for email-ability
- Have a PDF version (no animations) and a Google Slides version (for live edits)

## What investors actually do
- Look at slides 1, 2, 7 (problem, traction, team)
- Decide in 30 seconds if there's a meeting
- 5-min pitch is for nailing the 30-second decision
