## Naming conventions (what the category does)

- **Overwhelmingly descriptive "pat / patent / IP / clear" roots** — PatSnap, Patlytics, Patently, DeepIP, ClearstoneIP, FoundationIP, Patentfield, PatentPC. Crowded, legally weak, mostly taken.
- The **modern breakouts are NOT descriptive**: **Harvey** (person-name) and **Ironclad** (arbitrary-evocative word). Both distinctive, clearable, memorable.

## White-space (what nobody owns)

- **Audience:** every player targets **IP pros / law firms / corporate IP / creators**. **Nobody targets the solo AI builder / indie founder.** Wide-open.
- **Tone:** the category is serious/corporate/enterprise. An **approachable, builder-native, modern** voice (Vercel/Linear/Ironclad energy) stands out instantly.
- **Name:** a **distinctive, non-descriptive** name (the Harvey/Ironclad move) — *not* another "pat/IP/clear" word.

## Collision baseline — roots/names to AVOID

`pat*`, `patent*`, `*IP`, `clear*` (Clearbrief/ClearstoneIP), `snap`, `lytics`,
`deep`; and the marks: Patently, Patently-O, Harvey, Ironclad, Casetext, Clio,
Supio, Copyleaks, ScoreDetect, Polygraf, PatSnap, DeepIP, Patlytics, Edge.
